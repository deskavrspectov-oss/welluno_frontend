We’ve all said it: "I’m just having a busy week." But what happens when that "busy week" has lasted for six months?

In our previous discussion, we looked at the $1 trillion cost of burnout. But let’s get personal. Behind the global statistics are individuals sitting in home offices and cubicles, wondering if their exhaustion is a lack of "resilience" or something much deeper.

## The Anatomy of the "Slow Break"

A mental health disorder doesn't usually arrive with a bang. It arrives in whispers. It’s the "Invisible Mask" we discussed—the ability to look perfectly functional while your internal battery is at 2%.

How do you know if you're in the Grey Area?

- The Emotional Fog: You no longer feel "wins." A successful project launch doesn't bring joy; it only brings relief that it’s over.
- The Compassion Fatigue: You find yourself irritable with family or detached from colleagues.
- The Physical Echo: The tension headache that never leaves, the shallow breathing during every Zoom call, the inability to stay asleep past 4:00 AM.
